Associates of Perceived Quality of Life in Chinese Older Adults Living with Cognitive Impairment
Advance online publication, Journal of Gerontological Social Work, 19 Jul 2022
Xiang Gao, Kaipeng Wang, Fei Sun, Carson M. De Fries, Fang Xu, Peijun Huang & Yali Feng;

Abstract
The aim of this study is to examine perceived quality of life in Chinese older adults living with cognitive impairment and explore its associations with caregivers’ characteristics. Questionnaires were administered in person to 271 caregiver-care recipient dyads from urban communities in mainland China in 2019. We used the 40-item Alzheimer’s Disease-related Quality of Life tool and asked caregiver respondents to indicate care recipients’ life conditions. The questionnaire asked caregivers about their sociodemographic characteristics, levels of informal social support, caregiver burden, and depressive symptoms. Caregivers’ higher levels of caregiver burden (β = > −0.19, p < .01) and depressive symptoms (β = > −0.19, p < .01) amongst caregivers were significantly associated with lower quality of life among care recipients. Informal support from relatives and friends to caregivers did not significantly affect quality of life of care recipients. The results suggested that reducing caregivers’ burden and depressive symptoms are essential to promote quality of life of care recipients. Formal support from health professionals, service organizations, and communities are urgently called to promote the wellbeing of Chinese families affected by cognitive impairment.
Keywords: Perceived Quality of Life, Burden, Depression, Social Support, Cognitive Impairment

To Tell or Not to Tell: Investigation on Patient Attitude Towards Cancer Diagnosis Disclosure Based on Chinese Population
Qing Wu, Lijun Sun, Peijun Huang, Weijie Chen, Yanhong Zhang, Weixian Luo, Min Chen, Xianhe Xie;(September 2020)

Abstract
Objective: This study was designed to investigate the attitudes of patients to cancer diagnosis disclosure (CDD) and impact factors in China.
Methods: A structured questionnaire survey was applied to assess the preferences regarding to CDD of 403 cancer patients and 403 the relatives. The univariate and multivariate analysis were adopted.
Results: Compared with the relatives (67.99%), more patients prefer CDD (88.59%) and need encouragement from both relatives and physicians (82.88%). Majority of patients deemed that CDD could facilitate them to collaborate with their physicians (91.56%) and achieve a better efficacy (73.70%). In univariate analysis, significant factors associated with patient attitude toward CDD were as follows: cancer type (P=0.018), CDD notifier (P<0.001), notifier attitude (P=0.002), pattern of CDD (P<0.001), patient opinion on cancer (P=0.001), relatives attitude to CDD (P=0.040). Furthermore, multivariate analysis revealed that following factors held statistical significance: lung cancer (OR=0.119, P=0.006), stage I (OR=0.188, P=0.029), notifier: self-discovery of the cancer (OR=0.167, P=0.002), and patient opinion on cancer: calm (OR=3.387, P=0.018).
Conclusions: Patients are prone to accept CDD in China while relatives are reluctant to inform patients about their illness. The patient attitude to CDD may be affected by cancer type, stage, notifier and patient opinion on cancer.
Keywords: Attitude, Cancer Diagnosis Disclosure, CDD, Cancer, Relatives, Investigation



Association between Coping Strategies and Family Functionality in Caregivers of Childhood Leukaemia

Abstract
Objective: To explore the relationship between the coping style and family functionality of caregivers of children with leukaemia, and to provide a corresponding basis for social workers to intervene in the practical work of this group.
Methods: Data collected from 102 caregivers aged 35.29±5.246 in China were analysed. The Trait Coping Style Questionnaire (TCSQ) and the Family functionality score (Adaptation, Partnership, Growth, Affection and Resolve, APGAR) were used to analyse the relationship between coping styles and family functionality.
Results: In the low family functionality group (n=51), there was a significant difference between the number of children and the family functionality score (p<0.01); the disease stage of children and the positive coping score (31.02±5.023) of caregivers were significantly different (p<0.01). In the high family functionality group(n=51), there was a significant difference between the disease stage of children and the family functionality score of caregivers (p<0.05). There was no difference in the positive coping scores of caregivers of children with leukaemia between the two groups (p>0.05). The negative coping score (32.68±5.157) of the high family functionality group was lower than that of the lower one. The negative coping scores of the two groups were significantly different (P<0.01). Caregivers with lower family functionality easily choose the negative coping strategy (r=-0.366, p<0.01).
Conclusion: The caregivers of children with leukaemia with low family functionality tend to adopt negative coping styles. Social workers can choose the intervention strategies targeting the family level to improve their coping strategy, which can help them relieve the pressure on caregivers during the care process and improve their physical and mental health and the quality of care.
Keywords: Children with Leukaemia; Caregivers; Coping Style; Family Functionality
